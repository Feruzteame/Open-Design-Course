On the first week of September we received an email for an interview. It was on the 3rd of September. We start the day with introduction and collaborative assignments. The collaborative assignment was about the concept of freedom by Lloyd Morrisett. We were divided into small groups and doing a peer to peer learning and created a project together. After that we were asked to present our collaborative works. The next agenda was the interview. The interview was done individually. We were invited to a separate room and asked a general question. The language used for the course is English.The selected participants start the course on the 9th September. Only 15 people are chosen. The course will be held for about 3 months (until November). The first day of the course was fun and insightful. It was introducing about the open design concept as a whole. We learned also about soft tools and digital tools during the course. We discussed also about copy right, peer to peer learning methods, etc. Our course is a unique kind of learning environment. The teachers were coming from many different backgrounds. The learning environment was more of a sharing knowledge and ideas. Some of the subject we learned in the course ie Sound, HTML, Arduino, Blender, Narative Practices, The Screen Printing, The Book Cover experiment, Libre object workshop. The course was design to facilitate different kind of interest from the participants. It was very inspiring to meet with people who have different interest. The organizer also provided the participants with transportation reimbursement and lunch. Besides that they also borrowed us a laptop for us to work. We had opportunities to follow workshops and or projects outside the regular course in the weekend. For example the School of Love, Visible Project, and Sophia Conference. At the end of the course, each participant can chose any kind of projects suited with his/her interest. The organizer gave certain amount of fund to support the projects. This project will be shown on an exhibition on the last week of November. A certificate of attendance of the course will be given at the end of the course.

ARDUINO

facilitator: Wendy Van Weynsberghe and Hendrik Leper

Arduino is an open-source electronics platform based on easy-to-use hardware and software. It's intended for anyone making interactive projects. We look at exisiting projects and we start programming Arduino itself. Hello world, led blink!
material we used

  1. The Arduino Platform
  2. the Arduino border
  3. The Software (IDE)

Arduino is an open source physical computing platform based on a simple input/output (I/O) board and a development environment that is based on Processing. Arduino can be used to develop standalone interactive objects or can be connected to software on your computer, such as Processing, the internet with a.o. P5.JS with the p5.bots library, TouchDesigner, VVVV, Max, ...
Arduino is composed of two major parts: the Arduino board, which is the piece of hardware you work on when you build your objects, and the Arduino IDE, the software you run on your computer. In the IDE you write a sketch (a computer program with a set of instructions) that you upload to the Arduino board. This program tells the board what to do.
The Arduino board is a small microcontroller or, in other words, a small computer chip on a circuitboard. This computer is at least a thousand times less powerful than your laptop, but it’s a lot cheaper and very useful to build interesting devices. The programs you write for your Arduino are known as sketches. They are written in C/C++.

NARRATIVE PRACTICES

WEAVING A COLLECTIVE BODY THROUGH NARRATIVE PRACTICES
Facilitators: Hans Bryssinck, Elli Vassalou

The importance of sharing stories, of telling your story; not a finished one but something that needs to be recreated and re-told, done and un-done, over and over again:as an act of resistance, a way of building community, to rescue things and (re)build living memories,to process and exchange experience, to re-gain space for the person(al), the subject(ity),to create space again for listening to others, as opposed to imposing our stories and therefore our views. We use narrative language, to let different stories about how each one of us lives in this world, co-exist.

PEER 2 PEER ETHNOGRAPHY

from peer to peer to person to person
Facilitator: Penny Travlou in collaboration with Elli Vassalou

P2P Ethnography, as Ethnography, can be defined as a qualitative research design aimed at exploring cultural phenomena. Different from Ethnography, its aim is not to produce field studies or case reports, but to establish continuously available, accessible, participatory, performative and collaborative processes which allow gaining understandings about the knowledge and the systems of meanings in the lives of a social group, and its interactions with other ones.

ABOUT THE PROJECT

This project is not aimed to change the previous website but to give an additional content about the 2019 Open design course participant by putting it into the already existed website . The content will mainly tell about what is Open Design Course using this website. The aims of this project are:

  • To explain who are the Open design course 2019 participants
  • To explain what we learned in the course in the hope that the upcoming participant can have a clear expectations towards the course
  • Make a platform as a memory for the open design school 2019 participants

This documentary project is a collaborative effort from all of the open design course 2019 participants.We would like to thank you the participants for the interview and engagement during the process.
